The Best All-Inclusive Resorts for Teens Who Are Hard to Impress

Publish date: 2024-07-26
1

Castle Hot Springs  (Arizona)

Castle Hot Springs

This historic and all-inclusive Arizona resort is a brilliant getaway for groups with teens age 16 and up. Originally opened in 1897, the 1,100-acre natural hot springs resort offers a range of camp-like activities, including horseback riding, hiking, ATV tours, and Arizona's first via ferrata course (heads up that this one isn't included in your stay), in which daring adventurers can attempt to scale a 400-foot-tall peak.

2

Paws Up (Montana)

Stuart Thurlkill

Think of the massive, 37,000-acre Paws Up resort in Montana like a sprawling national park, but private (and oh-so-luxurious). Whether you stay in a luxury home or a safari-style tent, there are endless opportunities for outdoor activities to inspire teens—think cattle drives, ATV riding, fly fishing, a ropes course, go-karts, and so much more.

BOOK NOW

3

Lopesan Costa Bávaro Resort, Spa & Casino (Punta Cana, Dominican Republic)

Lopesan Costa Bávaro Resort, Spa & Casino

This Caribbean resort not only has a 16,000-square-foot Splash Island water park and its Vibe Teen Club, but it also boasts an on-site bowling alley, arcade, ice cream parlor, candy shop, and escape room. The best part? The 1,042-room resort is located along the white sand beaches of Punta Cana. We call that a win-win.

BOOK NOW

4

Villa del Palmar at the Islands of Loreto by Danzante Bay (Loreto, Mexico)

Villa del Palmar at the Islands of Loreto by Danzante Bay

This all-inclusive Mexican resort sits on the Loreto Bay National Marine Park, Mexico's largest marine preserve and a UNESCO World Heritage Site made up of five islands. Programming for teens and kids of all ages includes theme nights, Spanish lessons, Mexican crafts, paddleboarding, kayaking, and more. Plus, the area is teeming with exciting undersea life.

BOOK NOW

5

Falcon's Resort by Meliá (Punta Cana, Dominican Republic)

Falcon’s Resort by Meliá

Here, resort amenities meet entertainment experiences and unique interactive technology. All guests have access to the theme park, Katmandu Park, which is just steps away. Teens can also access BeyondME, the online game platform that lets them connect, personalize, and be rewarded for their engagement in both digital and real-world experiences. Offline, Bávaro Beach is a dreamy spot for a swim.

BOOK NOW

6

C Lazy U Ranch (Colorado)

C Lazy U Ranch

This all-inclusive ranch experience sits on 8,500 acres of land with more than 200 horses. Teens can enjoy whitewater rafting, a ropes course, cattle clinics, go-kart racing, riding horseback to the river for a swim, and even overnight teen campouts. During the summer program, each age group eats dinner separately from the adults.

BOOK NOW

7

Rise Up Surf Retreats (Costa Rica)

Rise Up

During retreats at these all-inclusive surf and yoga camps nestled on the coastlines of Costa Rica, teens can ride the waves, connect with nature, and build self-esteem—all in a relaxing paradise. Beyond surfing, Rise Up offers a range of culturally enriching activities.

BOOK NOW

8

Carlisle Bay (Antigua)

Carlisle Bay Antigua

Situated on a pristine bay surrounded by rainforest, Carlisle Bay has an all-inclusive booking option and tons of programs for kids of all ages. Teens can kayak, sail, windsurf, paddleboard, raft build, and snorkel—or even join a boat excursion to Sting Ray City. The resort is also home to a popular kids club, where tweens can go out on a Hobie Cat adventure or try their hand at mixology in a mocktail class.

BOOK NOW

9

Club Med Michés Playa Esmeralda (Miches, Dominican Republic)

Club Med

At this eco-conscious resort in Miches, Dominican Republic (the very first of its kind in the region), teens can join Club Med Passworld. The club offers a range of activities, like flying trapeze, windsurfing, hiking, and sailing, as well as the opportunity to socialize through group beach parties, dinner outings, and movie screenings on the beach under the stars. Plus, a special chill-out area in the patio lounge has suspended armchairs perfect for downtime.

BOOK NOW

10

Alisal Ranch (California)

Alisal Ranch

Situated in Southern California's Santa Ynez Valley north of Santa Barbara, Alisal Ranch is surrounded by mountains and sprawls across 10,500 acres. The luxury ranch experience offers stargazing, fly fishing, canoeing and kayaking on a 100-acre spring-fed lake, two 18-hole championship golf courses, six tennis courts, pickleball courts, and more.

BOOK NOW

11

Anse Chastanet (St. Lucia)

Anse Chastanet

Anse Chastanet is the sister resort to the adults-only Jade Mountain in St. Lucia, and the two share a 600-acre property with an on-site organic farm and open-air sustainable room design. The 49-room boutique hotel offers all-inclusive meal packages and an array of activities ideal for teens: Think jungle biking and hiking on the property's trails, farm-to-table cooking experiences, cacao harvesting and chocolate making, teen yoga and meditation classes, and snorkeling with sea turtles.

BOOK NOW

12

Hyatt Ziva (Cancun)

Hyatt Ziva Cancun

As the only resort in the region surrounded on three sides by the Caribbean Sea, this Cancun all-inclusive features swim-up suites and expansive infinity pools at the edge of a picturesque peninsula. Aside from the Moods Teen Zone—which has air hockey, table tennis, and a video game section—the resort also offers golf simulation, a dessert parlor, water sports, tennis, beach volleyball, pool parties, and trivia.

BOOK NOW

13

Rocking Horse Ranch (New York)

Rocking Horse Ranch

Located 90 minutes from New York City, this ranch resort has a packed slate of activities for everyone in the family. With horseback riding, an indoor water park, hot springs, mountain tubing, bungee jumping, mini golf, and other outdoor adventures, teens will be endlessly entertained. Options for lodging include bunk beds, so a family as large as six can fit in a single room.

BOOK NOW

14

Hyatt Ziva (Cap Cana, Dominican Republic)

Hyatt Ziva Cap Cana

Set on Juanillo Beach, Hyatt Ziva Cap Cana's water park has a lazy river with grotto, water cannons, and five slides. The Time Capsule is an exclusive area for teenagers with video games, a pool table, and foosball—and a 300-seat open-air theater offers nightly live performances.

BOOK NOW

15

Hilton Tulum Riviera Maya (Mexico)

Victor Elias

Hilton Tulum Riviera Maya recently unveiled its Family Zone, a secluded area for family members of all ages. Teenagers have a designated area within the zone, with a movie theater, air hockey table, billiards table, and the latest video games. If that's not enough, there's water volleyball, Ping Pong, and Spikeball too.

BOOK NOW

16

Beaches (Turks & Caicos)

Beaches Turks & Caicos

This Turks & Caicos all-inclusive resort is made up of five different villages, a 45,000 square-foot water park, 21 restaurants, a 12-mile beach, and 10 pools. For teens and tweens, there's an Xbox lounge, a teen disco, and a slew of other fun activities.

BOOK NOW

17

Sensira Resort & Spa (Puerto Morelos)

Sensira Resort & Spa

Sensira Resort & Spa overlooks a private beach with 359 rooms and suites, all of which have an ocean-view balcony or terrace. Offerings for all ages include spa services, a tennis court, ziplining adventures, a rock-climbing wall, three swimming pools—and even a fully-equipped movie theater and mini golf course.

BOOK NOW

18

Mohonk Mountain House (New York)

Mohonk Mountain House

A National Historic Landmark resort and Historic Hotel of America located in the Hudson Valley—just 90 miles north of New York City—this Victorian castle resort has something for all ages. For activity, teens can choose from hiking, horseback riding, tennis, art, archery, and more.

BOOK NOW

19

Grand Palladium Costa Mujeres Resort & Spa (Cancun)

Roberto Lara

Here, the whole family can enjoy three pools, a water park, and the Rafa Nadal Academy on-site tennis training program. Plus, the dedicated juniors club offers programming geared toward kids aged 13 and up.

BOOK NOW

20

VOMO (Fiji)

Monique De Caro

Teens can hike up Mt. VOMO to catch the first sunrise around the world—and get major social media bragging rights, of course. Meanwhile, divers can take to the crystal-clear blue waters with baby turtles and use snorkeling equipment, windsurfers, stand-up paddleboards, and kayaks to have fun on the water. For land experiences, there's a nine-hole golf course, as well as tennis courts and an array of cultural activities. Get there by direct flight from the U.S. West Coast.

BOOK NOW
