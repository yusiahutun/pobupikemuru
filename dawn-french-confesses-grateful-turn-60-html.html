Dawn French confesses she was 'grateful' to turn 60

Publish date: 2024-08-15

She's the hilarious comedienne who's experienced a successful career spanning decades as well as more than her fair share of heartbreak.

And after turning 60 last month, Dawn French has admitted celebrating the milestone birthday didn't faze her.

In an interview with Channel Seven's Sunday Night this week, the English actress revealed she was 'grateful' to make it this far.

'I am grateful to be here': In an interview with Channel Seven's Sunday Night, Dawn French admitted she was 'delighted to be 60' because many of her friends didn't make it that far

'Listen, I'm delighted to be 60,' she said, adding:  'I've got plenty of mates that didn't make it to 60, so I am grateful to be here.'

One of the loved ones she lost was her father Denys Vernon French, who took his own life when Dawn was a teenager.

Despite the pain of his death, Dawn told the program that she still feels his influence in her life today.

'Dad was very positive about me as a teenager and told me that I was gorgeous deserved the best,um, when it came to guys and stuff like that. And I remembered all that, you know, and that was my armour. Into my 50s, that armour has stood me in good stead,' The Vicar of Dibley star said.

Milestone: Dawn turned 60 last month and reflected on her life's difficulties and highlights 

Dawn also touched on her marriage to fellow comedian Lenny Henry, including their trying experiences as they attempted to conceive a child together.

'You have a kind of grief every time it doesn't work out, um, so we decided to abandon that and to adopt,' Dawn explained.

The couple, who wed in 1984, ultimately adopted their now 27-year-old daughter, Billie, in 1991.

'There's the big open, ignorant, cowardly attacks': Dawn explained she and her ex-husband Lenny Henry were subjected to vile racist abuse while they were married

Before they called it quits in 2010, Dawn and Lenny were subjected to disgusting racist abuse.

The actress and writer explaining how bad the racism had become at one point.

'There's the big open, ignorant, cowardly attacks that are things like excrement on your door. We had that. We had words scratched into every panel of every car, very offensive derogatory, racist terminology,' she said.

Dawn has since moved on from her broken relationship with Lenny and has since married Mark Bignell, 53.
