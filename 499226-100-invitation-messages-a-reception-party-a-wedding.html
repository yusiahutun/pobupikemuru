100+ invitation messages for a reception party after a wedding

Publish date: 2024-09-03

Not everyone invited to your wedding ceremony has to attend the reception party. You can cut down the guest list of the reception party to whatever makes you comfortable without feeling like you've left anyone out of the celebration. In this case, you need to send invitation messages for a reception party to the few people you want to grace the party.

In traditional weddings, the ceremony and reception are held on the same day. Nowadays, weddings have recently gone through a major facelift because these two events are treated as separate occasions. In some cases, they are held on different days. If you plan to go this route, you need to send reception-only wedding invitations. But how do you write a reception invitation?

Invitation messages for a reception party

If you're planning a private wedding ceremony with a large reception afterward, the wording is crucial. But how do you compose a message to invite someone to a wedding reception? These texts should serve as an inspiration if you have run short of words.

How to invite guests to a reception but not ceremony

The wording of the reception party should say that guests are invited to a reception in celebration of the marriage. This implies that you will already be married when the guests arrive for the party. If you are struggling to find the right words to invite guests to a reception, there is no need to panic. Check out these messages for inspiration.

  • As we have tied the knot already, the reception will follow at (place name) on (date). I hope you will join us for dinner and dancing.

  • We exchanged vows in a private ceremony on (date). Please join us for a celebration of our marriage on (date) at (venue).
  • We will be exchanging wedding vows on [date]. You're cordially requested to attend the wedding reception party that will take place on (date) at (venue).
