Nairobians in Distress as Floods Render City, Outskirts Impassable, Call for Action: &quot;Haiwork&quot;

Publish date: 2024-09-25
  • Nairobi residents are facing significant losses as heavy floods wreak havoc on their properties, rendering parts of the city and its outskirts impassable
  • Following heavy downpours on Tuesday night, the Kenya Red Cross has mobilised a team to assess the extent of the damage
  • Motorists have been issued warnings to avoid flooded roads for their safety as the agency initiates potential search and rescue

Nairobi is reeling from the devastating impact of heavy floods that have submerged vast areas, leaving residents in distress and calling for urgent action.

What damages did the heavy rains cause?

Following a night of relentless downpours on Tuesday, Nairobi residents woke up to scenes of destruction as floodwaters inundated streets, homes, and businesses.

The Kenya Red Cross Society has swiftly responded, deploying a team to assess the extent of the damage and potentially launch search and rescue operations.

"Last night's heavy downpours have caused significant flooding in various parts of Nairobi," announced the Kenya Red Cross Society. '"Our Nairobi Red Cross Branch team is on the ground, conducting assessments, and stands ready to assist those in need," it added.

Motorists have been issued stern warnings to steer clear of flooded roads to avoid endangering themselves and others.

Law enforcement agencies emphasised the importance of driving cautiously in wet conditions and seeking alternative routes if necessary.

Heavy rainfall to continue across the country

The Weatherman issued alerts predicting continued heavy rainfall events in several regions across Kenya.

These include the Highlands East and West of the Rift Valley, the Lake Victoria Basin, the Rift Valley, the South-eastern lowlands, the Coast, North-western, and North-eastern Kenya.

Gikungu, a meteorologist, has urged vigilance, highlighting the potential for reduced visibility and advising caution for road users, aviators, and boat operators.

"The anticipated heavy rainfall may be accompanied by strong winds, thunder, and lightning," cautioned Dr. Gikungu.

He further stressed the importance of avoiding shelter under trees and metallic structures to minimise the risk of lightning strikes.

As the flood crisis deepens, Nairobi residents are facing substantial losses of property and livelihoods.

Photos show flooding situation in Nairobi

Floods brought about by the heavy rainfall pounding most parts of Kenya resulted in deaths, human displacement, and massive destruction of property.

Though most parts of the country have been affected, the calamity strongly hit the Nairobi Metropolitan Area, which hosts the country's capital.

The situation saw Nairobi leaders, led by Senator Edwin Sifuna, Kibra MP Peter Orero and Kamukunji's Abdi Yusuf Hassan, call for help from the national government and other aid agencies.

According to the Centre for Affordable Housing Finance Africa, Nairobi County alone has a population of over 4.3 million residents and at least 1.4 million households.
