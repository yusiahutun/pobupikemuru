Gary Rhodes fans hail 'magic ingredient' Yorkshire Pudding recipe after chef's death

Publish date: 2024-04-26

Gary Rhodes has passed away at the age of 59.

Family and friends have been in mourning ever since, with scores of celebrity chefs paying tribute to the MasterChef star.

The British public is also deeply saddened by the news – and many have taken to social media to offer their condolences to the Rhodes family.

Gary will be remembered for his charismatic cooking tutorials and talent for British cuisine.

And by the looks of responses that have been springing up on Twitter, there’s one particular recipe that won't be leaving fans’ minds in the years to come.

On social media, Brits declared their love for Gary Rhodes’ Yorkshire pudding recipe.

Many have said it got them into cooking – with some saying they have been following his advice for decades.

One food fan remarked: “So shocked to hear of the passing of chef Gary Rhodes so young.

“His Yorkshire pudding recipe is the only one I use and would recommend to others.

“Thoughts with his family and friends at this sad time”

Another said: “Really sad news, when I was a young chef in catering college in Wales, he was the chef I strived to be. Still use his Yorkshire pudding recipe to this day.”

A third commented: “His Yorkshire pudding recipe is my go-to every time.”

And a fourth added: “Gary Rhodes’ Yorkshire pudding recipe is the best I’ve ever tried.”

In various books and TV appearances, Gary explained how to make the perfect Yorkshire pudding.

He took viewers through all the stages on an episode of New British Classics, which aired on the BBC.

The chef’s top tips include making the batter a day in advance and adding a “magic extra” ingredient.

He explained: “You’ve got to make a really thick batter – coating the ladle it’s so thick.

“You need to make that 24 hours in advance and they rise every time.”

The pro cook then took viewers’ through the ingredients list, explaining: “What you’re going to need is 225g of plain flower – and of course, all the other basic ingredients that go into a Yorkshire.

“There’s one other magical extra ingredient that does make it work. I’m going to add in three whole eggs and a magical extra is one egg white.

“Next you’re going to need some milk – between half a bottle and three-quarters of a bottle of milk. And just whisk it in…

“Now its best to add that half bottle first because it’s going to give you that really good, thick batter.

“And then in the morning if you find that it’s almost set, just add the remaining from the bottle and you’ve got your batter finished and made.”

To finish the recipe off, Gary added a generous pinch of salt.

He said this is important so you “get the maximum amount of flavour you can into the Yorkshire puddings”.

After giving the batter a whisk, he popped it into some tins that had been oiled and pre-heated.

These then crisp and rise up nicely as you place them in the oven for 20-25 minutes.

We're sure many will be thinking of Gary when they're making their roast dinners this week.
