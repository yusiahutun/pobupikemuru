Simeon Nyachae: Woman claims she got 2 children with ex-presidential candidate

Publish date: 2024-10-05

- Margaret Kerubo Chweya said she met Nyachae at Jogoo House where she had just been employed in 1973

- They two fell in love and would later conduct a traditional wedding ceremony in line with Abagusii traditions

- Their romantic relationship was blessed with two children in 1973 and 1980 before she moved to the US for studies

A Kenyan woman living in the US has claimed to be wife of the former Nyaribari Chache MP Simeon Nyachae.

Margaret Kerubo Chweya accused Nyachae's family of locking her and her two children out of the politician's funeral arrangements and the subsequent burial.

Speaking in Kisii county on Tuesday, February 16, Kerubo said she and Nyachae met sometime in 1973 at Jogoo House at a time he was still a civil servant.

At the time of their first encounter, Kerubo who was in the company of his son whom she claimed to be Nyachae's biological child, said she was working at the Jogoo House then.

The romantic affair with the then Cabinet minister was not secret as they conducted a traditional wedding ceremony in line with Abagusii customs since he had other wives.

After the wedding, she said they continued with the relationship and were blessed with two children Rodney Nyachae and Brian Nyandusi, who was named after Nyachae's father Musa Nyandusi.

Rodney was born in 1973, the same year the two had hooked up, while Brian in 1980.

As young enterprising woman, Kerubo now aged 65, disclosed that she managed to convince the former Ford People leader to facilitate her move to the US for her studies.

The woman said she arrived in the country from the US upon learning of Nyachae's ill health through his assistant and continued to stay around until he died on Tuesday, February 16.

The 2002 presidential candidate was laid to rest at his Nyosia home in Nyaribari Chache, Kisii county on Monday, February 15, in a ceremony attended by various dignitaries.

“It really hurt me when the family didn’t recognise me, I was not given priority like the inner family,” she added.

During the funeral service, Nyachae's other widow Grace advised young men to invest in their wives because they will be the only people who will stand by them in tough times.

"Young men, invest in your wives. When it became necessary, I was ready to look after Mzee because over the years he had invested in me. When you don’t invest in your wife, when your time of need comes, no one will be there for you" she said.
