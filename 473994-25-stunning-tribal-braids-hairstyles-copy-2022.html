25 stunning tribal braids hairstyles you should copy in 2022

Publish date: 2024-09-01

Tribal braids looks might be on trend, but in reality, braids have been part of style and culture for thousands of years across the globe. Historically, braids originated among African tribes as a sign of a woman's tribe, status, and social standing. Many of these braids were highly adorned, with silver coins or bits of amber woven among the braids.

It is easy to pick your next set of tribal braids if you are looking for an easy way to level up because different hairstyles are everywhere. These braids are perfect. Whether you're wearing knotless box braids or teeny micro braids, it's all thanks to the fact that beads come in many shapes, colours, and styles.

What are tribal braids?

Tribe braids are braiding patterns that have roots in African cultures. These braids often combine various braiding techniques with extra embellishments like beads, shells, and rings. The most common tribal braid designs are the Ghana braids, box braids, and Fulani braids.

Stunning tribal braids hairstyles

4. Side part tribal braids

A side-parted style is fun to change your appearance without significant changes. Additionally, you can leave the braids' ends out to highlight their curly nature. Side parted hairstyles suit square and diamond face shapes.

Frequently asked questions

  • How long do tribal braids last? They last up to 4-6 weeks, depending on the hairstyle. For example, the jumbo braids last four weeks, whereas the medium braid lasts 4-6 weeks.
  • How do you style tribal braids? You can style them any way you want.
  • Are tribal braids cornrows? The Fulani tribe in West Africa is where tribal braids first appeared as a way to commemorate the culture. Traditional versions of the style featured straight back against the head, middle-parted cornrow braids that were beaded.
  • Do tribal braids hurt? African women often braid their hair too tightly. So, it could hurt the first time you do it. However, the tribal braids should be comfy and not too tight when finished.
  • Are tribal braids a protective style? Yes, tribal braids are a terrific protective style as they leave your hair tucked in.
  • Are there men's tribal braids? Yes, there are different tribal braids for men to try out.
