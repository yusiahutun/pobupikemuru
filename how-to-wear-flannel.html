14 Flannel Outfit Ideas to Add to Your Fall Mood Board

Publish date: 2024-05-10

Of all the classic, cool-weather fashion trends we’re excited to see return this fall, flannel remains at the top of the list (joined by all-plaid-everything and oversized sweaters). The cozy staple is equally fashionable and functional, plus it's a natural fit for autumnal aesthetics from '90s grunge to Y2K normcore.

Here’s the thing about flannel: It’s a more versatile material than people give it credit for — especially once you consider all its color combos and fit options. Trust us, there are countless ways to wear flannel beyond pairing a plaid top with jeans, a white tee, and your fave booties.

So, whether you’re looking for a dressed-down outfit for fall activities, or are simply seeking something cute to lounge in, we’ve rounded up our favorite flannel outfits to try for fall.

With Boots and a Bomber Jacket

For a look that's equally cozy and chic, pair an oversized flannel shirt with a bulky bomber jacket and over-the-knee boots. Thigh-high boots double as a way to keep your legs warm so you can skip pants altogether or toss on a pair of tights and bike shorts.

With a Maxi Skirt

Oversized flannels don't just pair well with maxi skirts. Thanks to the long lines of each piece they create an unexpected and unique silhouette when worn together.

With Denim

No shade to lumberjacks, but dark wash denim and a red flannel shirt while classic is definitely a familiar 'fit come fall. Breathe new life into the jeans-and-flannel combo by picking a colorful top, black jeans, and a pair of great ankle boots.

Dress It Up with Heels

Add a feminine sensibility to a flannel by wearing it with strappy heels. A pair of simple, streamlined cropped bottoms or bremuda shorts will highlight your heels while playing nicely with your plaid print.

With Corduroy Flares

Channel '70s glam vibes by tucking your flannel into a pair of cozy, high-waisted corduroy flares. Sticking to a similar color palette creates a cohesive flannel outfit, but you can also color-match based on tone by pairing warm or cool shades.

With Leggings

Leggings are the perfect bottoms to wear with a flannel shirt since their close fit creates a streamlined silhouette. Add crew socks and fresh kicks for a casual outfit that can be elevated with a sleek leather tote.

Cropped with Fun Pants

A change in season doesn't always mean a change in temp, but that doesn't mean you can't still wear a flannel. For balmy days and nights, work a cropped flannel into your 'fit rotation. For an evening out, we highly recommend a pair of leather fun pants.

Mix Prints

Believe it or not, flannel is a great pattern to mix with other prints, especially when you work in differing textures as well. If you're worried about clashing, keep your flannel tucked in and toss a cardigan on top.

Break Up an All-Black 'Fit

There's no denying that all-black is chic, but this monochromatic approach to dressing is only enhanced when worn with a flannel shirt in an unexpected shade.

As a Matching Set

What looks high-fashion, but feels like pajamas? A matching flannel set. The high-low blend of rugged boots, sleek black accessories, and a sweatshirt tied at the shoulders, prep-style, tones down the flannel. The same balance can be achieved with a long trench or oversized blazer.

With a Mini Skirt

Another flannel outfit that’s perfect for in-between weather is wearing your shirt with a mini skirt. Since the material is heavier than your typical button-down, it’ll be just as warm as a sweatshirt so you can go bare-legged.

As a Poncho

We never thought we'd be extolling the fashion virtues of a poncho, yet here we are. A tunic-length flannel poncho, cinched at the waist with a statement belt, is, dare we say it, the moment?

Add a pair of plaid shorts underneath and you've got yourself a major look.

With White Pants

Just because you throw on an oversized flannel doesn’t mean your bottoms have to be form-fitting. Don’t be afraid to go baggy throughout, skipping slim bottoms and pulling on a pair of comfy wide-leg pants, in a refreshing shade of white. For a casual feel, opt for running shoes over anything with heels, or grab your strappy stilettos to dress up the look.

With Matching Bottoms

When in doubt, color coordinate. A monochromatic 'fit is definitely a statement look, but it's one we love to make, especially with flannel.
