Certificate in Primary Education requirements in teachers colleges in Kenya

Publish date: 2024-06-14

Teaching is one of the most rewarding career choices. If you aspire to become a primary school tutor, there are several courses that you can pursue. So, what are the Certificate in Primary Education requirements in teachers' colleges in Kenya?

Certificate in Primary Education, or the P1 Certificate, is a course designed to train students to become professional educators. The knowledge and skills impacted on them make them well-suited to teach in private or public primary schools.

Certificate in Primary Education requirements

Previously, persons interested in becoming primary school teachers undertook a P1 course. However, with the implementation of the Competence Based Curriculum (CBC), these teachers ought to pursue a diploma in primary education.

The new Diploma in primary education course has phased out the certificate in teaching requirements. What is the minimum qualification for a P1 teacher? To enrol for this course, you must meet the requirements for a teaching certificate in Kenya:

TSC qualifications for a primary school teacher in Kenya

  • At least C (Plain) in the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Examination (KCSE).

There are no additional requirements, unlike in the past, where the prospective teacher trainees were required to have the following:

  • At least C (Plain) in the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Examination (KCSE) with a C- in two art subjects or languages plus a P1 teacher certificate.
  • Be a Kenya Advanced Certificate of Education (KACE) holder with one principal and two subsidiaries.

Subjects offered to P1 teachers

Since the phasing out the certificate of primary education course, the government, through the Ministry of Education, has introduced cluster groups for subjects to be pursued. Therefore, qualified teacher trainees must specialize in three subjects from the following clusters.

Cluster one

  • Kenya sign language
  • Indigenous languages
  • Foreign languages (French, German, Chinese and Arabic)

Cluster two

  • Mathematics
  • Agriculture
  • Home Science
  • Science and Technology

  • English
  • Kiswahili
  • Physical and Health Education

TSC requirements for primary teachers

For the Teachers Service Commission to consider employing you as a primary school teacher in Kenya, you must satisfy the following minimum Certificate in Primary Education requirements:

  • Not more than 45 years old when applying for employment
  • You must be a Kenyan citizen
  • You must be a holder of a PTE Certificate (P1 certificate) from KNEC or,
  • A bachelor's of education degree in primary education from a university recognized by the Kenyan government with two teaching subjects.
  • Diploma in Primary Education application

Since the certificate of primary education was abolished, the application process for those seeking to pursue a diploma in primary education has been simplified. After meeting the minimum requirements, you can apply directly to the Teachers Training College of your choice.

Where can I work with a certificate in primary education?

The course allows teacher trainees to pursue careers in various fields, such as:

  • Education Administrator 
  • Primary/Secondary School Teacher 
  • Community Education Officer 
  • Youth Worker

  • What are the Certificate in Primary Education requirements in Kenya? All KCSE certificate holders with a mean grade of C (plain) are eligible. There are no additional entry requirements.
  • Can you do a certificate in P1 with D+? No. The minimum requirement for a diploma in primary education is now C (plain) in the Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE).
  • How long does a certificate in Primary Education take? The curriculum design stipulates that the program typically takes three years to complete.
  • Why was the Certificate in Primary Education phased out? The certificate in primary education program was phased out due to the competency-based curriculum's introduction and the need for teachers with higher educational qualifications.
  • What is the difference between a certificate in primary education and a diploma in primary education? Unlike the certificate in primary education, where trainees took all subjects, they will specialize in their areas of interest and qualification for a diploma in primary education.
  • What is the entry grade for a Diploma in primary education? The entry grade for a teacher trainee hoping to pursue a Diploma in Primary education is C (plain) and above in KCSE.
  • How much does a Kenyan primary teacher earn per month? Most primary school teachers earn between Ksh 26,065 and Ksh 135,946 monthly in 2023.
