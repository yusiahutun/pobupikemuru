Dominique Fishback is tired of playing the “easy-to-like” character. No wonder, she declined the role of “Marissa” and chose to be the central antihero, Andrea “Dre” Green, in Amazon Prime’s new series Swarm.

Keep reading to find out more.

How Much Is Dominique Fishback Net Worth?

Dominique Fishback flaunted a net worth of over $1 million in 2023.

An actor by profession, Dominique started her career in 2014 in the TV series The Knick and The Affair. The next year, she then had a chance to grace quite a few shows such as The Americans, Blue Bloods, Royal Plans, and Show Me a Hero.

But it wasn’t until 2017, she got her first recurring role as “Donna ‘Darlene’ Pickett” in the TV series The Deuce. Thereafter, she landed a role as “Najja” in Random Acts of Flyness (2018), “Robin” in Project Power (2020), “Deborah Johnson” in Judas and the Black Messiah (2021), “Lil” in Modern Love (2021), “Robyn” in The Last Days of Ptolemy Grey (2022), and now, she’s portraying “Dre” in Amazon Prime’s new series Swarm.

Swarm is a comedy-horror series that featured hows Dre’s obsession with a fictional, Beyoncé-like pop star, Ni’Jah (Nirine S. Brown), along with her love for her foster sister, Marissa (Chloe Bailey), lead her down a violent path.

But initially, Dominique was actually recruited for the role of Marissa, not Dre! “I’m used to being a character that’s loved or easy to like and, no lie, you get comfortable with that — I like that!” Fishback revealed ahead of Swarm’s March 17 premiere. “But I had to get out of my own way to be the artist, the actor, that I came here to be.”

Thus, she opted to portray Dre.

Recalling the interaction when Dominique first asked for the role, Donald Glover said, “I was like, ‘You want to play the murderer? Uh, really?’ She felt very sweet, like someone you wanted to take care of, but she wanted to do the opposite, which I completely understand even in my own career. She wanted to branch out, and I was excited to allow her to do that.”

Thanks to Dominique’s dedication as an actor, she even made it to Forbes’ “30 Under 30 – Hollywood & Entertainment” list in 2019.

Did you know: Dominique was a member of the Opening Act for the 2017 and 2018 Play Reading galas, being featured as both an actor and a playwright in the latter.
