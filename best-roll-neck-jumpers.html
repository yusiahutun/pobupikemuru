Roll neck jumpers for women

Publish date: 2024-09-21

We earn a commission for products purchased through some links in this article.

Prada

Ever since working from home and Zoom meetings became a regular part of our lives, we've been trying to have as much fun with casual clothes as possible – so why not swap your branded T-shirt for a jumper this winter? Prada's is made from wool and cashmere, detailed with subtly puffed sleeves and intarsia knitted with the brand's old-school Milano logo across the chest.

Moncler Genius + JW Anderson

Moncler Genius + JW Anderson

Ribbed knitwear will hold its shape better than most – no one wants a saggy roll neck. What's more, this JW Anderson + Moncler Genius piece has lace-up detailing that allows you to control the fit. Let a flash of hazard yellow peak out from beneath your wrap coat or bomber jacket this winter.

£55 Arket

£55 Arket

Sometimes, instead of burrowing inside an oversized jumper, you need one to underpin your outfits. Knitted from Merino wool which is breathable on bare skin, Arket's is ideal for layering. Let the cream iteration complement this season's Eighties-inspired cardigans and preppy V-neck knits, or invest in the black version for pairing with slip dresses.

Ermanno Scervino

Ermanno Scervino

Ermanno Scervino's roll-neck jumper is knitted with silk to create a skintight silhouette that layers effortlessly. But it's the colour we're coveting the most: neon fuchsia will give everything from khaki skirts and blue jeans to black leather trousers a new lease of life.

Paige

Who said roll neck jumpers couldn't be alluring? Paige's has a dropped shoulder silhouette, ribbed detailing and is slashed at the décolleté to showcase a hint of skin.

Rag & Bone

Rag & Bone

There's nothing quite like the appeal of an oversized jumper with an invitingly capacious roll neck. Rag & Bone's camel version has dramatically wide sleeves and a unique contrast overstitching along the arm seams. Wear it with cashmere joggers for lounging in, then switch to wide-leg trousers and trainers for a smarter (but no less cosy) look outside of the house.

Ganni

If you've done knitwear to death, look to update your basics with subtle details like the tie-fastening waist on this Ganni jumper. It's even smarter than it first looks: the wrap will hide the inevitable lumps and bumps that result from tucking and layering, as well as providing myriad styling options.

Victoria Beckham

Victoria Beckham

Speaking of layering – if minimising fuss and streamlining your silhouette is a priority, we suggest investing in a bodysuit asap. Victoria Beckham's is made from 100 per cent Merino wool, so it looks just like a jumper when paired with leather skirts, jeans and tailored trousers.

Sacai

Sacai's chunky wool jumper is defined by its asymmetric side slit and diagonal zip fastening. But it's the slouchy shape and elongated sleeves that make this piece feels so effortless when paired with wide-leg trousers and joggers.

Reiss

A basic building block of the staple wardrobe (along with a trench coat and Breton top), everyone needs a slimline cashmere roll neck in their repertoire. Reiss' super soft offering comes in all the essential neutrals including black and camel.
