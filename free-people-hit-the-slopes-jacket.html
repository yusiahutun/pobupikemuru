Kaia Gerber and Hailey Bieber Wear Free People Hit the Slopes Jacket

Publish date: 2024-05-18

In the summer, celebs pare back their wardrobes, wearing basics like their $530 Golden Goose sneakers. In the winter, they keep warm by bundling up in toasty outerwear like their $950 Canada Goose coats. And while celebrity closets are generally packed with pricey pieces (and informed by a possible goose fixation — we're looking into it), one of their most popular picks for fall costs just $148.

Free People's FP Movement Hit The Slopes Fleece Jacket has won over A-list fans Kaia Gerber and Hailey Bieber thanks to its cozy, breathable material and versatility. In fact, both stars are so taken by the jacket that they each own it in multiple colors, and they seriously can't stop wearing them.

Gerber was first spotted in the jacket this spring when she stepped out in a beautiful lavender color that Free People calls Jammin, which she proceeded to wear three times in one month. Since then, she's acquired the jacket in Eclipse navy blue and Cardinal red, the latter of which she has already worn twice in the last month.

Bieber, also a fan of the Cardinal, was seen in the jacket as recently as two weeks ago when she posted a selfie wearing the fluorescent green Lime Zest version. Unlike Gerber, the model also owns the fleece in printed form, opting for an abstract print called Black Toile Combo.

Hundreds of customer reviews back up our assumptions about the jacket, which has earned a 4.5 star average rating. Shoppers, who've gone so far as to upload a rainbow of selfies in all the jacket's many colors, have called it their "most worn piece of clothing," "so warm and soft," and "the most comfortable thing I've ever owned."

"After weeks of trying to decide if I needed this jacket, I went for it and couldn't be [happier] that I did," wrote one reviewer. "It is the perfect jacket. It would fit any and all body types and is comfy and cute. Perfect for running around town or for a quick hike or even dinner with your friends!"

With 17 colors and eight patterns to choose from, the only tricky thing about this jacket is deciding which one to start with — but as many fans attest to, you can always come back for more.
