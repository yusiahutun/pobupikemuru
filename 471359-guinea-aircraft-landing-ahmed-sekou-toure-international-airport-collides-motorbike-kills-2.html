Guinea: 2 Killed As Aircraft Landing at Conakry Airport Collides with Motorbike on Runway

Publish date: 2024-08-29
  • Two men were sucked into the engine of an aircraft that was landing at the Ahmed Sékou Touré International Airport in Guinea
  • Airport authorities explained that the pair was trying to cross the runway on a motorbike when the incident occurred
  • Despite the aircraft rolling on without further incident, its return trip was cancelled as investigations into the incident begin

A TAP Air Portugal Airbus A320neo killed two individuals after it collided with a motorbike while landing at Ahmed Sékou Touré International Airport, Guinea.

Details indicate that the engine on the right side of the aircraft hit the motorbike that was near the runway at the time of landing.

The two were trying to cross the runway when they were sucked in by one of the engines of the aircraft that had just landed.

Publico reports that the accident occurred at 11:40 pm when flight TP1492 originating from Lisbon hit the two individuals and obliterated the motorbike.

The aircraft rolled out without further incidents and came to a complete stop several yards away from the accident scene.

A report from the management at Ahmed Sékou Touré International Airport noted that passengers and crew were all fine.

“All safety procedures were followed, but even so, it was not possible to avoid the aforementioned accident,” it continued.

Airline and airport issued condolences to affected families

The management added that the identified driver was a security guard, an employee of a company in charge of securing the aircraft fence.

“The driver identified was a security agent, an employee of a company in charge of protecting the airport facilities”, read the report.

Both airline and airport expressed their condolences to the families involved.

The return flight TP1493 to Lisbon was canceled as the Civil Aviation Authority of Guinea (AGAC) with the support of the Department of Security and Protection (SOGEAC) launched an investigation into the incident.

TAP Air also confirmed that it is collaborating with local authorities to investigate the circumstances that led to the regrettable situation.

“The tragedy and the opening of an investigation prevented the Portuguese company from boarding the passengers who were supposed to leave for Lisbon,” the report concluded.
