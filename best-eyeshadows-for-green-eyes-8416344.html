The 12 Best Eyeshadows for Green Eyes of 2023

Publish date: 2024-08-03

With more melanin than blue eyes and less than brown ones, green eyes sit right in the middle, emitting warmth and vibrancy from their gaze. Green eyes are the rarest eye color out there and stun as they boast a variety of tones, such as golden brown and rich emerald. But to draw even more attention to them, you can lean on eyeshadow shades that help them shine.

“If you want to get the most ‘pop’ payoff, we have to go back to the color wheel,” says celebrity makeup artist Emily Amick. “Colors that are opposites create the most contrast, which means that green eyes will stand out most when they are surrounded by colors in the red family. Aim for shades with a reddish, warm undertone to really contrast your green. This could be auburns, pinks, corals, or rusty browns. I also love a warm golden shimmer with green eyes.” Celebrity makeup artist Tobi Henney also adds that “plums and navy look great on green eyes.”

From soft crimson to cool plum, we’ve got 12 fabulous options the pros love to use on their green-eyed clients below.

What to Keep in Mind

Formula + Finish 

Eyeshadows come in finishes ranging from matte to shimmer. You’ll often find a mix of many finishes in one palette, but some are strictly one, so pay attention to your options. Choosing between powder, cream, or liquid depends on your preferred application method. Grab a cream or liquid for quick application or powder to create more intricate looks.

Finding Your Perfect Fit

Just because these shades all work well on green eyes, that doesn’t mean you have to like them. “It’s all about finding what works well for your own preference and style,” says Scibelli. “If you find that you're not a rose gold or maroon fan, green eyes also work really well with coppers, champagnes, and golds.”

Your Questions, Answered

How can you change the intensity of a color?

Just because a shadow is buildable, doesn’t mean you have to build it up. “These tones can be worn sheer or bolder,” says Scibelli, so play with them! If you want to go bold, cream and liquid formulas tend to go on heavier than powder. To make a powder stand out more, wet your brush a bit before dipping it into the palette.

Why should you still wear whatever shadow color you like?

“Playing with color is never that serious,” says Amick. “Instead of contrasting colors, try matching your eyes with a similar green shade for a cool monochromatic moment. Throw in some yellows or blues. Who cares?! You can always take it off if you change your mind!”
