15 Best Eye Creams for Dark Circles of 2024

Publish date: 2024-06-30

Why is it that nicknames referring to dark circles are always so cute? Panda eyes and raccoon eyes make them sound endearing, but when you’re facing those shadows in the mirror, they’re anything but. And while layers of concealer can help, they’re only masking the issue. To really solve your dark under-eye anxiety problem, you need a heavy-hitting eye cream in your skincare routine, yet so many of them just don't cut it.

We decided that we were done looking tired when we actually had a lovely night's rest, and we spent five weeks — and 744 hours! — testing 22 of the most popular eye creams for dark circles. We evaluated each for how well they brightened and neutralized our dark under-eye hues, along with the formula's consistency, texture, and absorption. We compiled our notes to score and rank each eye cream, editing the list down to our very favorites. After thoroughly testing each one for five weeks, these are hands-down the 15 best eye creams for dark circles that made us look bright-eyed and well-rested every day (even when we weren't).

What to Keep in Mind

  • Active Ingredients: Lisa Chevalier, M.M.S., PA-C and founder of SoVous says vitamin K is fantastic for dark circles caused by leaky blood vessels and inflammation, and it can also help with rosacea and wound healing. She also suggests looking for formulas with vitamins C and E, as they're potent antioxidants that protect the delicate skin around the eye; vitamin C also can help boost collagen production and brighten discoloration.

    “Caffeine contains vasoconstrictive properties, which means it can temporarily reduce puffiness and improve blood circulation to reduce the appearance of dark circles,” says Dr. Ariel Ostad, a board-certified dermatologist, adding that niacinamide's anti-inflammatory properties also work wonders to brighten hyperpigmentation.

    If you're looking to tackle fine lines along with dark circles, “retinol will stimulate the production of collagen in the skin," says Chevalier, but make sure retinol-based creams also contain soothing ingredients, like collagen-building peptides, to keep the formula balanced.

  • Texture: Similar to facial moisturizers, someone with dry skin should opt for a richer cream that provides intense hydration, while someone with oily skin should steer clear, and opt for a thinner formula or even an eye serum that taps gel-like ingredients, like niacinamide, over butters. Sensitive skin types should use a fragrance-free product with fewer ingredients, ideally gentle ones that are plant-based.

    All skin types will benefit from a humectant-rich eye cream, like hyaluronic acid and glycerin, as well as emollients, such as ceramides, all of which are gentle ingredients. These skincare heroes will help keep the delicate eye area deeply hydrated and looking plump, ultimately improving the appearance of dark circles.

    Lastly, remember that your skin may change seasonally; many people have formulas that they rotate in and out of their routine as the weather shifts. This concept should apply to your eye cream as well. 

  • Your Lifestyle: Board-certified dermatologist Dr. Margarita Lolis tells her patients to start using an eye cream once they hit their mid- to late twenties “as a preventative measure to maintain cutaneous health around the eyes,” she says. “Many creams are formulated with sunscreen and antioxidants.” 

    Sleepless nights are a major contributor to dark circles, as are poor skin habits. If you’re prone to burning the midnight oil (and your skincare routine suffers as a result), Dr. Ostad suggests a vitamin C or caffeine-filled product, or an eye cream specifically formulated for puffiness to help reduce inflammation and dark circles.

Your Questions, Answered

What causes dark circles around the eyes?

“Some common causes include things that we can't avoid, such as genetics and normal aging,” says Chevalier, adding that allergies can also play a role. “Lifestyle habits such as smoking, lack of sleep, poor diet, and lack of sun care can all lead to premature aging around the eyes and, consequently, dark circles.”

If you’re dehydrated or tired, you may also notice dark circles because of the blood vessels in the area, which become engorged, making their blueish color more visible under thinning skin.

Do eye creams actually work for dark circles?

All of our experts agree that eye creams can improve the appearance of dark circles, but it’s important to have reasonable expectations. You’ll also have to do your part by maintaining a healthy lifestyle. “Proper care for yourself is what matters most in treating under eye circles,” says Chevalier. "Eating a balanced diet full of fruits, veggies, whole grains, healthy fats, and lean meats will go a long way, as will sleeping well and drinking plenty of water — and, of course, wearing SPF daily." 

Can dark circles be permanently removed by cream?

This totally depends on the cause of your dark circles. If your circles are caused by allergies, poor sleep, or dehydration, a targeted eye cream can improve their appearance, but it will be more helpful to avoid allergens, get more rest, and drink more water. If your shadows are caused by aging, you likely won’t see a dramatic improvement, though adding skin-strengthening and hydrating ingredients will help.
