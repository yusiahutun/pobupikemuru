Luke Perry biography

He was born on October 11th, 1966 in Mansfield, Ohio in the United States of America. He was the son of the late Coy Perry Jr., a steelworker and his wife, Ann Perry. Unfortunately, he never got the pleasure of being brought up by both parents as they divorced when he was only six years old, and his mother gained custody over him and his two siblings. Ann later got married to Steve Bennett, a construction worker. He spent the most substantial fraction of his young life in the custody of his mother and stepfather in the village of Fredericktown. The young Luke attended and graduated from Fredericktown High school. During his time there, he was the school mascot.

Career and achievements

His acting journey commenced when he moved from Fredericktown to Los Angeles in 1984 at the age of 18 years. It was not easy at the start because he took a string of odd jobs to fend fees for his acting classes. He worked as a paver at construction sites. He would then suffer numerous rejections in auditions but still pursued his passion. As a majority would put it, a legend who labors for long, labors for a reason. Luke Perry's first breakthrough happened in 1987 when he earned his first acting role in Loving. He featured as Ned Bates in the daytime Television drama from 1987 to 1988. The move would later unfold as the beginning of so many projects to come.

How did Luke Perry die in Riverdale?

The actor landed a steady project on CW's Riverdale, as Fred Andrews. The teen drama show based its storyline on a longtime comic book franchise. After first appearing in January 2017, the show attracted steady viewers, thanks to Fred's father and son's special moments. However, after his untimely death, there was concern about how the actor's death will be addressed.

To dispel doubt, Roberto Aguirre-Sacasa confirmed to Altpress that the show was to address the death of Luke in their fourth season as Molly Ringwald was slated to replace him in the show. Luke featured last for Riverdale in season 3 episode 19.

When did Luke Perry pass away?

The actor suffered the first massive ischemic stroke attack on February 27th, 2019 while at his home in Sherman Oaks, Los Angeles. He was then rushed to the hospital before he suffered a second stroke a couple of days later. He then died on March 4th, 2019 at Providence Saint Joseph Medical Centre in Burbank at the age of 52 years. Luke Perry's death was a shock not only to the filming industry but also to his large number of followers who were still expecting more from him.
