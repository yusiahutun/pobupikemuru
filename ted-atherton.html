Early Life of Ted Atherton

Ted Atherton was born in 1962 in Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada. He is Canadian and belongs to the White ethnicity. He attended the University of Toronto and earned his bachelor's degree in English and Drama. Later, he joined Banff Centre and studied there for three years.

Ted Atherton's Career

Ted Atherton made his acting debut in 1998 from a CBC television show, Nothing Too Good for a Cowboy in a lead role, Panhandle Phillips. The same year, he guest starred in TV series La Femme Nikita and Tv movie Rescuers: Stories of Courage: Two Families. 

The following year, Atherton appeared in The City, Joan of Arc and Switching Goals. In 2002, he starred in the Canadian/American television series, Sue Thomas: F.B.Eye as Myles Leland III.
