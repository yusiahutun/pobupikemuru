Quincy Jones rejected Marilyn Monroe for breast shape

Publish date: 2024-02-26

Marilyn Monroe might be a sex icon to the masses, but not to Quincy Jones.

In an interview with GQ that was published on Monday, the 84-year-old record producer revealed his friend Frank Sinatra wanted to set him up with the bombshell.

But Jones refused as he said he thought the Some Like It Hot actress 'had a chest that looked like pears.' 

Red carpet! Quincy Jones, seen in November, told GQ that he passed on a date with Marilyn Monroe in the early 1960s

She was the hottest thing in town: Here Monroe is seen at the Golden Globe Awards in March 1962; she died five months later. Quincy is seen around that same time

'The big bands, that's like the school of the dogs,' he said as he tried to explain how women threw themselves at him.

'Traveling bands? Every f***ing night it was like the girls coming through [department store] Neiman Marcus, "Oh, I like trumpet players," "I like sax players," "I like guitar players"... Rita Hayworth, all of them.

'It was unbelievable, man. Frank was always trying to hook me up with Marilyn Monroe, but Marilyn Monroe had a chest that looked like pears, man.'

Marilyn was one of the biggest sex symbols during the 1950s with films like The Seven Year Itch. She died in an alleged suicide in August 1962 after her divorce from legendary playwright, Arthur Miller.

A big star: The blonde beauty starred in several hits such as Some Like It Hot, Niagara and Diamonds Are A Girl's Best Friend

His look: Jones is seen here in leather slacks in the 1960s; at the time he was producing music

When Jones was specifically asked to clarify his rejection of Monroe, he told GQ: 'Let's not talk about it. Come on, man. We killed it.

'You know, I came up with the two wildest motherf***ers on the planet. Ray Charles and Frank Sinatra. Come on. They were good-looking guys, they had all the girls they wanted, and they showed you how to deal with it.'

Jones' romantic history is varied, as he has been married three times.

He was wed to Peggy Lipton from 1974 until 1990; to Ulla Jones from 1967 until 1974; and to Jeri Caldwell from 1957 until 1966.

He is also a father to seven children.

When the famous producer was asked why he was a womanizer, he said: 'I don't know. Probably because I didn't have a mother.'

Famous pals! Jones was friends with many stars; here he is pictured in 1994 with legendary Rat Pack singer Frank Sinatra

As the famous composer talked about his masculine influences, he brought up Dominican diplomat, sportsman, and playboy, Porfirio Rubirosa.

'[He was] the king of the playboys. 

'Amazing man. What a guy. Eleven-inch dong. And in Paris to this day, you go to Chez L'Ami Louis, the waiter will come over to you with a pepper shaker and say, "Here's your Rubirosa."

Friendly exes: Despite their 1990 divorce, Jones kept things friendly with ex-wife Peggy Lipton. left, who he shares daughters Rashida Jones, second to left, and Kidada Jones, far right

'He always used to say, "Quincy, it's by the head, not the bed. Women give up p***y to get love, men give up love to get p***y." That's the way it works. You know, all these women were available all over the world. 

'I did a tour with Nat Cole in '61 with my band - we couldn't stop the girls. It's incredible. Women are a trip, man.'

Despite being such a womanizer, Jones is a family man and frequently attends events with his daughters.

Earlier in January, the music legend was seen with his daughter Rashida Jones at Stella McCartney's Autumn 2018 collection launch in Hollywood.
