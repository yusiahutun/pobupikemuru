Early childhood

James Duke Mason was born on April 27, 1992, in Cedars Sinai, Los Angeles, California, but the family moved to France when he was two years old. The 30-year-old activist is the only son of singer Belinda Carlisle and film producer/former White House official Morgan Mason. His grandparents are the late British actor James Mason and media personality Pamela Mason.

Duke grew up in France and was schooled there. He came out as gay in 2006 when he was fourteen, and his parents showed support for him. He became the first openly gay president of his international high school’s student council in Mougins, France. In 2010, Duke graduated from high school and moved back to the United States.

Career beginnings

During the summer of 2008, Duke was appointed to serve as a Page in the United States House of Representatives. He studied political science at California Lutheran University and then moved to West Hollywood, where he got involved with grassroots advocacy.

During his early career years, he had a stint in acting by being featured in a small role in the film What Happens Next. The film was screened as part of the Hollywood Film Festival. In 2010, Mason was recognized as among the most influential young LGBT Americans in The Advocate’s “Forty Under 40” issue.

In the following year, he was also named in OUT magazine’s 2011 “Out 100” issue among the 100 most influential LGBT people in the world.

LGBT advocacy

Due to his work on LGBT issues, Duke has appeared in many TV shows as a guest. These shows include Nancy Grace, Dr Phil, and Larry King. He also had the opportunity to serve as an official surrogate for the Barack Obama 2012 US Presidential Election campaigns as well as Hillary Clinton’s 2016 campaign.

Between 2012 and 2015, he was a member of Outfest’s Board of Directors, making him the youngest member to serve in that capacity. Having gained some political experience, Duke decided to run for the West Hollywood City Council during the March 2015 election. Although he was unsuccessful, Mayor Lindsey Horvath appointed him to the 21st Century Leaders Task Force.

2015 brought another milestone when Duke got the opportunity to serve as Special Guest Grand Marshal of the Sacramento Pride Parade. At the same time, he was on the Board of the West Hollywood Community Housing until 2017. For the next three years, he was on the West Hollywood LGBTQ+ Advisory Board and was also a Co-Chair.

Political ambitions

In 2019, Belinda Carlisle’s son once again ran for West Hollywood City Council but was more prepared than the first time. He emerged fifth out of 11 contestants. He had been endorsed by longtime Councilmember John Heilman, US Congressman Sean Patrick Maloney, and the LGBTQ organization Equality California.

As a freelance writer, he has produced pieces for major publications like The Advocate, The Huffington Post, The Hollywood Reporter, OUT Magazine, Queerty, and LGBTQ Nation.
