Blaine Gabbert | Football Career

Upon completing his high school degree, Blaine joined the University of Missouri in 2008. The same year, Blaine appeared in the US Army All-American Game.

Professional Career

After graduating from the university in January 2011, Blaine announced entering the 2011 NFL Draft.

After playing for the NFL draft Matt Waldman, the blogger of The New York Times, ranked Gabbert as the No one quarterback prospect in the draft.

On July 28, 2011, Blaine signed a four-year contract with Jacksonville Jaguars under the coach Jack Del Rio.

After playing against Indianapolis, Colts Blaine led the team to win the match with a 22-17. 

Unfortunately, after Blaine suffered from a right forearm injury, he was placed on the injury list, due to which he officially ended the 2012 season.

After recovering from the injury, the quarterback was back for the Jaguars’ 2013 season but only played three games.

After Jacksonville Jaguars, Blaine was traded to the San Francisco 49ers on March 11, 2014. Gabbert officially made his debut on the 49ers by playing against Denver Broncos with a 42-17.

Blaine continued his 2016 season with the San Francisco 49ers, where he was also named a starter quarterback player. Not to mention, his arm strength is quite commendable and, according to coach Bruce Arians, “hard to compare.“
