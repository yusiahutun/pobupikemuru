UCLA football player awarded record $35 MILLION after motorcycle accident

Publish date: 2024-03-18

A former UCLA football player and fitness model has been awarded a record $35 million in a personal injury lawsuit, after a motorcycle crash caused him to lose his left leg.

Nick Ekbatani, 29, was driving his motorcycle on the North Pacific Coast Highway in Redondo Beach, California when he crashed with a taxi driver in July of 2012.

The intersection has been declared 'negligently designed' and the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans) will be responsible for 70% of Ekbatani's compensations.

A rise in the roadway at the intersection causes a 'line of sight problem' for drivers and has caused 'numerous' accidents over the years, according to KTLA 5.

As he reached the intersection of North Pacific Coast Highway and Diamond street, a taxi failed to yield to the right-of-way while turning left, causing the crash. 

The jury who awarded him the record breaking restitution were shown a video of the painstaking process of cleaning and fitting Ekbatani's prosthetic leg, and told of his 13 surgeries following the accident.

In the years since the accident, Ektatani has become a motivational speaker, and teaches fitness classes

Ekbatani revealed the horrific details of what he remembers from the crash, and the years of struggle he endured coming to terms with his amputation.

He previously testified: 'I'm thinking there's nothing I can do, I'm going to die,' according to LA West Media.

'It's something that was hard for me to figure out what was real and what was imaginary.'

 Ekbatani said he was grateful for the verdict, and to be alive and walking 

He continued: 'Out of nowhere, there was an obstruction in my right-of-way,'

'I don't recall the specifics, but I do remember sensations, extreme panic.'

Caltrans testified that there was an 'oversight' on their part in the lack of double yellow lines extending into the intersection, which engineers were ordered to install in 2005, that would stop vehicles from turning left on to Diamond Street too early. 

Following the verdict, Ekbatani said: 'It's been four-and-a-half years, obviously it's been up and down, ebb and flow, completely nonlinear.'

But, I'm just grateful to be on the other side of things, walking, and just so grateful to be alive, and obviously grateful for the verdict.' 

His former fiancee Kelli Tennant testified on his behalf at the trial. The two met shortly before the accident and were later enagaged, but the stress of caring for Ekbatani caused Tennant to call off the plans for the wedding.

'Since day one, she was like an angel,' he said of Tennant. 

'I'm grateful to her. She saved my life.'

In the years since the accident, Ektatani has become a motivational speaker, and teaches fitness classes. He was formally a marketing coordinator at ESPN and recently received a Masters in Business from UCLA.
