Jeremy Renner: Sister reveals the actor continues to positively recover in the hospital

Publish date: 2024-02-16

Jeremy Renner's sister, Kym Renner, gave an update to People in an issue published on Tuesday that her brother is 'crushing all the progress goals.' 

This comes after he was crushed by a seven-ton snow plow near his Lake Tahoe, California cabin on New Year's Day.

His sister further expressed that both she and other close family members are 'thrilled' with the Hawkeye actor's recovery and added, 'We couldn't feel more positive about the road ahead. If anyone knows Jeremy, he is a fighter and doesn't mess around.'

The beloved star, who celebrated his 52nd birthday in the ICU (intensive care accident) on Saturday, underwent two surgeries in order to place metal pins around his legs, along with chest surgery, after being left in critical condition following the frightening incident. 

Along with his caring sister, Jeremy's friend and fellow actor, Colin Farrell,  additionally gave a recent update on the star's recovery status.

The Irish actor, who previously co-starred with Renner in S.W.A.T., told Entertainment Tonight on Sunday, 'I've been in touch. He's doing good, I believe,' adding, 'All prayers are with him.' 

Critical condition: A 911 emergency call log, obtained by TMZ, showed that a call was made after the actor was run over by the snow plow machine indicated that the right side of the actor's chest collapsed and his upper torso was crushed

The actor had been helping a family member with a personal vehicle that was stuck in the snow, Washoe County Sheriff Darin Balaam said during a news conference held last week on Tuesday, two days after the accident. 

He used the 14,330-pound snow plow to help the family member before getting out to speak with them. During the conversation, 'it was observed that the PistenBully [snow plow] was starting to roll,' said Balaam. At that point, Renner tried to get back in and was run over.

The sheriff added that police do not suspect foul play, nor does it suspect Renner was at all impaired at the time. He said that neighbors 'came out with towels' and gave him first aid, though none were doctors.

'Doing good': Colin Farrell, fellow actor who had previously worked with the Marvel star in the past, told Entertainment Tonight on Sunday, 'I've been in touch. He's doing good, I believe,' adding, 'All prayers are with him'; seen on Sunday in NYC

The same day of the conference, a source had informed DailyMail.com that Jeremy was awake and talking. 

A 911 emergency call made after the actor was run over by the snow plow machine indicated that the right side of the actor's chest collapsed and his upper torso was crushed.

The emergency log, obtained by TMZ, included notes from the accident that said Renner could be heard moaning in the background of the call and was experiencing heavy bleeding from his head and other injuries.

The injuries to his chest and torso were reportedly making it difficult for the Marvel star to breathe before he was airlifted to a nearby hospital from the scene of the accident at about 10am on New Year's Day near his home in the Reno area.

Since his hospitalization, Jeremy has shared his own personal updates to his fans and followers on social media, which began with a selfie the star had taken while resting in the ICU. 

Notable bruises could be seen on his face, and the actor penned in the caption, 'Thank you all for your kind words. Im too messed up now to type. But I send love to you all.' 

He later shared a clip of himself being pampered by his supportive sister and mother as he received a relaxing scalp massage. 

'A "not no great" ICU Day, turned to amazing spa day with my sis and mama. Thank you soooooo much,' he had penned in the caption of a tweet. 

Over the weekend on Saturday, Jeremy celebrated turning 52 while still in the hospital, and uploaded a photo onto his Instagram story the day before as he paused for a picture with his medical team that have assisted him on his recovery journey.
