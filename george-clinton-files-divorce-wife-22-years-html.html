Funk legend George Clinton has filed for divorce after more than two decades with his wife, Stephanie Lynn Clinton.

According to TMZ, the 71-year-old - who has been estranged from his wife for some time - filed divorce documents in Florida.

Not so funky: George Clinton has filed for divorce from his wife Stephanie after 22 years of marriage

TMZ reported on Saturday that the documents explained the couple have 'experienced long term separation' throughout the marriage and that George is seeking a divorce as 'the marriage is irretrievably broken.'

Not sudden: The musician, who was inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame last year, has been separated from his wife for some time

Standing testament to the couple's long-term separation, the court papers said they no shared property and no shared bank accounts.

The pair also have no children together - George had a son from a previous relationship, George Jr, but he passed away in 2010 aged 50 after suffering from liver disease.

Stephanie was the P-Funk All-Stars front man's second wife and she worked as his personal assistant and manager.

The couple got married in 1990 in Toledo, Ohio,  and lived on a farm in Michigan prior to their separation from which they were evicted.

George is no stranger to a court of law having struggled to regain the rights to his songs after selling them to a company to avoid going bankrupt and he also sued The Black Eyed Peas claiming they sampled and used his music without proper permission.

He claimed the group used elements of his 1979 song Knee Deep in remixes of their international hit Shut Up.

George and the band settled out of court for an undisclosed amount in 2012.
