F1 ace delights MotoGP and Valentino Rossi fans with Sky Sports interview

Publish date: 2024-08-12

F1 ace Lando Norris is a massive fan of MotoGP hero Valentino Rossi and wants the whole world to know about it.

The British star couldn’t resist donning some official merchandise ahead of the Italian Grand Prix.

As the cars lined up on the grid at Monza, the Sky Sports cameras collared Norris for a chat.

To the bemusement of the presenting team, the 19-year-old was proudly sporting his Rossi bucket cap.

Presenter Simon Lazenby asked Norris if we wanted him to look after the garish head gear.

Norris, however, curtly refused the request as he scurried towards his McLaren.

Fans on social media were loving the youngster’s style.

“Lando Norris is just awesome. Greater @ValeYellow46 fan and lives it out right on the track,” said one.

“Watching the F1 paddock wear black sunglasses and sponsored watches. Lando Norris: Rossi bucket hat,” another added.

“Lando ‘Rossi fan boy’ Norris,” commented a third.

“Lando Norris is addicted and crazy about @ValeYellow46.” observed one more.

Carlos Sainz Jr eventually swiped Norris’ beloved cap, much to his irritation.

Norris’ profile picture on Twitter also shows him clutching a giant cut out of Rossi’s number 46 emblem.

The 19-year-old was in Valentino Rossi MotoGP gear during a video games session with Max Verstappen which was streamed live earlier this month.

“Lando, what the f*** are you wearing,” Verstappen said during the session.

“It’s, uhh… Valentino Rossi merch. Valentino Rossi merch, nothing wrong with it…”

Earlier this week, Norris slammed Jacques Villeneuve’s claims that young drivers’ regular use of simulators makes them prone to take more risks.

“I don’t think it’s got anything to do with sim racing,” Norris told Motorsport.com .

“It’s just something he maybe wants to use as an excuse for it.

“Safety is getting much better, especially compared to when he would have been racing.

“It’s not like we completely forget it. We don’t go flat out and not care about anything. We still realise what danger is.”
